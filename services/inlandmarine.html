Inland Marine Insurance

Protect your tools, equipment, and cargo wherever your business takes them.

Overview

Despite its name, Inland Marine Insurance has nothing to do with the ocean. It is a flexible commercial policy that covers property in transit, tools, equipment, and specialized assets that move from location to location. Standard commercial property policies only cover items at a fixed business location — Inland Marine fills that gap.

What It Covers

  • Contractors' tools and equipment while on job sites, in transit, or in storage.
  • Mobile equipment such as cranes, forklifts, and heavy machinery.
  • Cargo and freight in transit (including goods shipped by truck, rail, or air).
  • Electronic and computer equipment that travels with your employees.
  • Fine arts, exhibits, and valuable items transported for shows or events.
  • Installation floaters for materials and equipment being installed at a customer's site.

Who Needs Inland Marine Insurance?

  • Contractors and construction companies with tools and equipment on multiple job sites.
  • Trucking and freight companies moving goods for customers.
  • Businesses that ship products to customers regularly.
  • Event planners, photographers, and media companies transporting expensive equipment.
  • HVAC, plumbing, electrical, and other trade contractors.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does my commercial property policy cover my tools at job sites?

Standard commercial property policies typically only cover property at your listed business location. Inland Marine covers property away from your premises.

Is Inland Marine the same as cargo insurance?

Inland Marine is broader — it can include cargo insurance but also covers tools, equipment, and other mobile property. Cargo insurance is specifically for goods in transit.
